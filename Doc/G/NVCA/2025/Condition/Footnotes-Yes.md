23.sec=An important point of negotiation is often whether the Company will represent that a given fact (a) is true or (b) is true to the Company’s knowledge. Alternative (a) requires the Company to bear the entire risk of the truth or falsity of the represented fact, regardless of whether the Company knew (or could have known) at the time of the representation whether or not the fact was true. Alternative (b) is preferable from the Company’s standpoint, since it holds the Company responsible only for facts of which it is actually aware. Alternative (b) is also most compatible with the role of diligence and the Company’s representations in venture capital transactions, which is to ensure that venture investors have the best information available regarding their potential investment, rather than to “shift risk” in the manner of an M&A transaction (in other words, venture investors do not expect to sue portfolio companies or their founders for statements made in good faith that later turn out to have been incorrect).  <a href="{!!!}Def.Knowledge.sec">§2 (Knowledge)</a>
